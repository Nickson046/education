Admissions

What academic qualifications do I need to apply?
Requirements vary by university and programme, but in general you'll need:
  • A secondary school certificate (Form 6 / A-Levels or equivalent) for undergraduate programmes
  • A bachelor's degree for master's programmes
  • English proficiency (IELTS 6.0+ or equivalent) for English-taught programmes
  • Some medical programmes require chemistry and biology at A-Level
Our advisors will review your specific qualifications in your free consultation.
When are the application deadlines?
Most Baltic universities have two intakes: September (main) and February (limited programmes). Key deadlines:
  • September intake: Applications open January–May, deadline typically June 1–July 1
  • February intake: Applications October–November
We recommend applying at least 6 months before your intended start date to allow time for document preparation and visa processing.
Is English enough or do I need to learn Latvian/Lithuanian/Estonian?
All our partner universities offer full degree programmes taught entirely in English — no knowledge of Baltic languages is required. Some universities offer optional local-language courses which can be helpful for daily life, but these are never mandatory.
Can I apply to multiple universities at once?
Yes, and we encourage it. Most of our packages include applications to 3–5 universities, increasing your chances of acceptance. We help you build a balanced list of reach, match, and safety schools across Latvia, Lithuania, and Estonia.

Visa & Immigration

What visa do I need to study in the Baltic states?
You will need a national long-stay visa (Type D) followed by a temporary residence permit for students. The process involves:
  • Submitting your visa application at the relevant embassy
  • Providing proof of university acceptance
  • Showing sufficient financial means (typically €3,000–€5,000)
  • Health insurance coverage
Our visa specialists manage the entire process and prepare you thoroughly for your interview.
How long does the visa process take?
Processing times typically range from 4–10 weeks. We recommend beginning your visa application at least 3 months before your programme start date. Our 94% visa approval rate reflects our thorough preparation process.
Can I bring my family with me?
Yes, after completing your first year and obtaining your residence permit, you may apply for family reunification. Your spouse and dependent children under 18 may join you. They will need their own residence permits and must demonstrate sufficient financial support. Speak to our visa team for details.
What happens to my visa after I graduate?
All three Baltic states offer post-graduation job seeker visas. In Estonia you can stay for 9 months to find work; Latvia and Lithuania offer similar provisions. Upon finding employment, you can convert to a work permit. EU residency becomes an option after 5 years of legal residence.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

What scholarships are available for African students?
Several scholarship sources are available:
  • University merit scholarships — 10–50% tuition reduction for strong academic performance
  • Baltic government scholarships — offered by the Latvian, Lithuanian, and Estonian ministries of education
  • Erasmus+ and EU funding — available for certain programmes
  • Private foundations — we partner with several diaspora-led foundations supporting East African students
Visit our Scholarships page for full details.
Can I work while studying?
Yes. EU regulations allow international students to work up to 20 hours per week during term time, and full-time during holidays. Average student wages in the Baltics range from €6–€10 per hour. Many of our students cover a significant portion of their living expenses through part-time work.

Costs & Finances

How much does it cost to study in the Baltics?
The Baltics are among the most affordable EU study destinations:
  • Tuition fees: €2,500–€8,000 per year depending on programme and university
  • Accommodation: €200–€450/month (student dorms €120–€250/month)
  • Food & living: €300–€500/month
  • Total annual budget: Approximately €7,000–€14,000 per year
This compares very favourably with the UK (€25,000+/year) or Germany (€12,000+/year).

Student Life

Is it safe for African students in Latvia, Lithuania and Estonia?
The Baltic states are generally safe and welcoming. All three capitals have growing international student communities. Like anywhere, it's important to be street-smart, and isolated incidents do occur. Our alumni network and on-ground liaison team provide a strong support community. Most of our students report very positive experiences and feel at home within a few months.
What is the weather like?
The Baltics have a temperate continental climate. Winters (November–March) are cold, with temperatures ranging from -5°C to -15°C and snowfall. Summers are pleasant (20–25°C). Many African students find the first winter the biggest adjustment — we provide a complete packing guide and seasonal preparation tips to all enrolled students.
Is African/halal food available?
Yes. Riga, Vilnius, and Tallinn all have African restaurants and halal-certified supermarkets, particularly in areas near university campuses. Most large supermarkets stock international ingredients. Our student communities also organize regular communal cooking events where students share foods from home.
